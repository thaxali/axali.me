## Goal

Find prominent designer portfolios and adjacent design references from Apple, OpenAI, Airbnb, and the Jony Ive / LoveFrom orbit. The goal is not to copy their sites. The goal is to understand how high-craft designers present themselves, especially when their public work is confidential, institutionally complex, or spread across many domains.

Ax's MVP direction:

> A highly designed, text-first, single-page public dossier. Wikipedia function, editorial execution.

The reference question:

> How do elite designers make themselves feel legible, tasteful, credible, and worth betting on without over-explaining?

## Primary References To Study

## 1. LoveFrom

URL: https://www.lovefrom.com/

Connection:

- Founded by Jony Ive with Marc Newson.
- Current OpenAI hardware/design adjacency through the Jony Ive / OpenAI io relationship.

Why study:

- Extreme minimalism.
- Almost no conventional portfolio evidence.
- Brand confidence through restraint.
- The site proves taste by withholding, not explaining.

Design choices:

- one-screen-ish identity
- tiny amount of text
- high typographic confidence
- no case-study grid
- no marketing language
- calm, almost ceremonial presentation

Useful for axali.me:

- restraint
- confidence
- "less, but better" energy
- type as interface

Danger for axali.me:

- too little information would fail Ax's legibility problem
- Ax needs proof and story, not only aura

## 2. Mike Matas

URL: https://classic.mikematas.com/

Connection:

- Apple iPhone / iPad interface work.
- Facebook Paper / Instant Articles.
- Nest Thermostat.
- LoveFrom.

Why study:

- A short canonical bio does most of the work.
- The site is effectively a compact proof index.
- It has confidence without a long personal brand apparatus.

Design choices:

- very short bio
- work presented as a linked proof list
- personal texture appears briefly, not as a huge memoir
- low-friction, no over-explaining

Useful for axali.me:

- the first 200 words matter
- selected work can be presented as canonical proof
- compactness can feel more serious than excessive case-study prose

Danger for axali.me:

- Ax needs more narrative spine than Matas because the whole problem is understanding the layers

## 3. May-Li Khoe

URL: https://www.maylikhoe.com/

Connection:

- Former Apple designer/prototyper.
- Worked on early iPhone/iPad/web, Force Touch, Taptic Engine, Find My iPhone/Friends, and related interaction work.
- Later VP of Design at Khan Academy.

Why study:

- Strongest analogue for "person who does not fit into one category."
- Combines Apple credibility, invention, teaching, writing, art, culture, workshops, and public voice.
- Does not flatten into a conventional resume.

Design choices:

- long-form personal page
- voice-forward
- many identity layers in one scroll
- clear public availability for speaking, workshops, writing, and collaboration
- specific stories and side quests make the person vivid

Useful for axali.me:

- validates that a polymath page can be long if it has voice
- public work, personal story, talks, workshops, and writing can coexist
- "not fitting a category" can be a strength if handled directly

Danger for axali.me:

- visually less aligned with Apple/Jony simplicity
- axali.me MVP should be more designed, structured, and canonical

## 4. Kenneth Kuh

URL: https://kennethkuh.info/

Connection:

- Public portfolio includes OpenAI projects and Apple/WWDC/iPhone work.
- Also has Nike and editorial/brand work.

Why study:

- Strong example of confidentiality-aware portfolio architecture.
- Uses work index, project titles, images, and "confidential" labels without over-explaining.
- Shows how to make serious work feel real even when details cannot be fully disclosed.

Design choices:

- text-forward project index
- dense work list
- repeated horizontal separators
- minimal explanation
- selective images
- confidentiality noted plainly
- identity/contact visible without a big hero

Useful for axali.me:

- project lists can be elegant and credible
- "confidential" does not need to weaken proof if handled with confidence
- dense archive style could fit the designed Wikipedia direction

Danger for axali.me:

- too much list density without narrative would not solve Ax's coherence problem

## Cross-Cutting Portfolio Patterns

## Pattern 1: The Best Sites Do Not Over-Explain The First Screen

Common choices:

- name
- role
- current work
- one proof line
- minimal nav

Implication for axali.me:

The hero should not do all the biography. It should create orientation and desire to continue.

## Pattern 2: Elite Portfolios Treat Institutions As Proof, Not Logos For Decoration

Good sites say:

- what role
- what contribution
- what product/system
- what changed

Implication for axali.me:

Apple, Amazon, Adobe, Bilt, Itaú, UW, and Seena should appear with context, not as a random logo strip.

## Pattern 3: Confidentiality Can Be Designed Around

Kenneth Kuh and Bradley Haynes show that confidential or semi-private work can still strengthen credibility if framed plainly.

Implication for axali.me:

Use:

- "confidential"
- "selected contribution"
- "public summary"
- "outcome"
- "what this proves"

## Pattern 4: Career Timelines Are Useful, But Not Enough

OpenAI designer pages and read.cv profiles are great for role clarity. They are not enough for Ax because the page needs story and worldview.

Implication for axali.me:

Use a timeline as one module, not the whole site.

## Pattern 5: The Most Relevant References Are Not Purely Minimal

LoveFrom and Zach Johnston are too sparse for Ax. May-Li Khoe is closer conceptually because she has many layers, but too loose visually.

Implication for axali.me:

Target hybrid:

- LoveFrom restraint
- Mike Matas compact proof
- May-Li Khoe layered humanity
- Kenneth Kuh archive density
- Bradley Haynes selected-work clarity
- Shaun Modi impact summaries
- Karri Saarinen systems thinking

## Pattern 6: "Designed Wikipedia" Is Still The Right MVP Direction

The references point to a useful synthesis:

- canonical facts from Wikipedia / read.cv
- editorial voice from May-Li
- proof index from Mike Matas / Garrett Olinger
- selected work and outcome framing from Bradley Haynes / Shaun Modi
- extreme typographic restraint from LoveFrom

## Design Moves To Borrow For axali.me MVP

1. One-sentence identity at top.
2. Sparse top nav with page anchors.
3. Designed infobox for canonical facts.
4. Short lead summary before anything visually complex.
5. Work entries as proof cards, not decorative thumbnails.
6. "What this proves" labels for selected work.
7. A career/proof timeline that can be scanned.
8. Explicit serious-inquiry CTA.
9. Media/speaking/publishing assets in the same page.
10. Strong typographic contrast instead of visual noise.

## Design Moves To Avoid

1. A logo wall without explanation.
2. A generic product-designer case-study template.
3. Overly sparse LoveFrom-style withholding.
4. Overly personal long-scroll without structure.
5. Portfolio thumbnails that do not explain why the work matters.
6. Too many animated interactions before the story is clear.
7. Persona-specific landing sections above the whole-person case.
