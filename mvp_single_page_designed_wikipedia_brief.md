## MVP Thesis

The MVP of axali.me is not a full portfolio, not a blog, not a multi-page personal brand site, and not the full RAG-backed agent experience.

The MVP is:

> A highly designed, text-first, single-page public dossier that makes Ax legible as a rare person worth betting on.

The model is not "Wikipedia aesthetic." It is "Wikipedia function, editorial execution."

In other words:

- encyclopedic clarity
- strong hierarchy
- factual density
- timeline and proof
- internal cross-references
- source-backed claims where possible
- highly intentional typography and layout
- enough personality to feel unmistakably Ax

The page should feel like:

> If a serious person tried to understand Ax in five minutes, this is the page they would wish existed.

## Primary Audience

The MVP serves the collapsed persona:

> The high-agency opportunity-maker.

This includes investors, hiring leaders, publishers, agents, editors, workshop buyers, university hosts, future teammates, press producers, and senior introducers.

Their core job:

> When I encounter Ax, I want to understand the pattern, proof, and future potential behind his range, so I can decide whether to spend my access, reputation, money, time, attention, or social capital on him.

## MVP Goal

The MVP should help a high-agency visitor answer six questions quickly:

1. Who is Ax?
2. Why is his range coherent?
3. What has he actually done?
4. What does he believe?
5. What is he building / thinking about now?
6. Why should I act, introduce, invite, hire, invest, publish, or follow?

## What The MVP Must Do

### 1. Be Carryable

A visitor should be able to describe Ax in one or two sentences after reading.

Working carryable explanation:

> Dr. Ax Ali is a Creative Scientist who builds applied AI products and human-centered systems. His work translates research, design, and technology into products, companies, stories, and experiences that help people understand, create, and connect.

### 2. Collapse Breadth Into Pattern

The page must make breadth feel like accumulated power, not scattered identity.

Working pattern:

> Ax translates imagined or unresolved human needs into lived systems.

### 3. Prove Seriousness

The page must include enough evidence to make serious people comfortable acting.

Proof types:

- Ph.D. in Human-Computer Interaction
- publications and awards
- Apple / Amazon / Bilt / Itaú / Adobe-style institutional proof
- product launches
- AI and accessibility work
- Seena/current company work
- talks/workshops/writing
- testimonials where available

### 4. Show Taste

Because the page is text-first, the design quality matters even more.

Taste signals:

- controlled typography
- confident whitespace
- editorial pacing
- precise language
- no inflated claims
- no generic startup copy
- no random "personal brand" decoration

### 5. Create Next-Step Paths

The single page should not be a dead end.

It should route to:

- invest / partner
- hire / rare roles
- book a workshop
- invite to speak
- publishing / editorial
- press / media
- join / build with Ax
- follow / read / contact

## 4. Lead Summary: The Wikipedia-Style Opening

Purpose:

- provide the canonical 2-3 paragraph version of Ax
- make the page quotable and shareable

Draft shape:

```text

Across research labs, big tech, startups, and founder-led work, Ax has built systems that help people communicate, adapt, learn, create, and connect. His projects span AI-powered accessibility research, mixed-reality interaction, grocery and loyalty products at scale, design-led innovation workshops, and Seena, his current company.
```

Design:

- text block with line measure optimized for reading
- strong first sentence
- optional inline annotations or citations

## What Is Excluded From The MVP

The MVP does not need:

- full multi-page IA
- live RAG agent
- full case studies
- blog archive
- CMS
- animations
- complex visual storytelling
- all testimonials
- all project images
- deep SEO system
- separate investor/hiring/publishing/workshop pages

## What Makes It Highly Designed If It Is Mostly Text

Design levers:

- typographic hierarchy
- editorial grid
- structured infobox
- margin notes / anchors
- proof footnotes
- tight line lengths
- intentional section pacing
- one signature accent color
- high contrast statement block
- elegant table of contents
- dense but breathable metric grid
- link styling that feels archival / canonical
- mobile-first reading rhythm

The design should feel like:

- Wikipedia's authority
- a museum wall label's precision
- an editorial profile's pacing
- a founder memo's clarity
- a personal manifesto's voice

## Success Criteria

The MVP works if a high-agency visitor can:

1. Understand Ax in under 60 seconds.
2. Explain Ax to someone else in one paragraph.
3. Believe the range is coherent.
4. See enough proof to reduce risk.
5. Feel there is a bigger worldview.
6. Know what action to take.
7. Want to keep the tab open or forward it.

## Recommended MVP Build

One static single page.

Could be:

- hand-coded HTML/CSS
- Framer single page
- Next.js single route

The full future site can come later. The MVP should prove the core experience:

> Can a designed text page make Ax legible and compelling enough that high-agency opportunity-makers want to act?
