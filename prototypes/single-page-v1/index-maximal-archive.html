01 / Story

Some people specialize by narrowing. Ax specializes by translating across worlds.

From a childhood blueprint that became a family home, to interpreter tools, accessibility research, AI-native products, and company building, the pattern is consistent: imagined or unresolved human needs become lived systems.

Origin points
Age six

Sketches into artifacts

At a kitchen table in Baghdad, clothing designs became the first felt proof that an idea could leave the mind and enter the world.

Fifth grade

Blueprint as translation

A child redraws a floor plan to help his parents communicate a spatial idea. The architect builds from it. Design becomes lived reality.

College

Language into care

Repeated hospital interpreting trips turn into an app idea: a phone passed between caregiver and patient to reduce friction in care.

UMBC

HCI clicks

An elective, a lab, 3D printers, laser cutters, electronics, and accessibility research create a home for making ideas real.

Research

Technology that adapts

Projects in Parkinson's, dyslexia, accessible interaction, and machine learning point toward systems that adapt to people.

Now

AI for shared understanding

Seena extends the same thesis into AI-native products for insight, customer understanding, and human decision-making.
