## Decision

The primary audience for axali.me is not one job title, industry, or persona. The primary audience is:

> High-agency opportunity-makers.

These are people who can materially change Ax's trajectory because they have access to capital, roles, stages, institutions, networks, publishing channels, teams, or cultural distribution.

The site should make one big case:

> Ax is a rare person worth betting on.

This does not mean the site should be boastful or generic. It means the site should help serious people quickly understand the pattern, proof, and possibility behind Ax's range.

## Who Counts As A High-Agency Opportunity-Maker

This includes people who can:

- invest in Ax or Seena
- hire Ax into rare roles
- publish Ax
- put Ax on stage
- bring Ax into universities, labs, or institutes
- join Ax's company
- introduce Ax to rooms he should be in
- partner with Ax or Seena
- commission Ax to teach, facilitate, advise, or create
- amplify Ax's ideas through media, conferences, podcasts, or editorial platforms

## The Visitor's Real Question

Different visitors arrive with different surface questions:

- "Should I invest?"
- "Should I hire him?"
- "Should I publish him?"
- "Should I invite him to lecture?"
- "Should I book him for a workshop?"
- "Should I join his company?"
- "Should I introduce him to someone important?"

Underneath, they are all asking a deeper question:

> Is this person worth using my access, reputation, money, time, or attention on?

axali.me needs to answer yes with story, proof, clarity, and taste.

## What The Site Must Prove

The site must prove four things.

### 1. Pattern

Ax's range is not randomness. It has a throughline.

Working throughline:

> Ax translates imagined or unresolved human needs into lived systems.

This pattern shows up in:

- childhood clothing sketches
- the fifth-grade blueprint story
- the pocket compass and early mentor
- language interpretation in healthcare
- building his first computer
- HCI and accessibility research
- adaptive interfaces
- AI-native products
- company building
- workshops and teaching

### 2. Credibility

Ax has earned serious trust across serious rooms.

Proof categories:

- Ph.D. in Human-Computer Interaction
- peer-reviewed publications
- award-winning research
- Apple / Amazon / Bilt / Itaú / Adobe-style institutional proof
- product launches
- AI and HCI project depth
- testimonials from respected people
- Seena and founder work
- teaching / workshops / public thinking

### 3. Taste And Judgment

The visitor should feel that Ax does not just do many things. He makes strong choices.

Taste signals:

- how the site is written
- how proof is curated
- how visual design handles restraint and richness
- how personal story is treated without melodrama
- how AI is discussed without hype
- how work is explained through systems, not vanity metrics alone

### 4. Future Potential

The site is not only a museum of what Ax has done. It should create conviction about what Ax can do next.

The visitor should leave thinking:

> I want to be connected to whatever this person builds, writes, teaches, or leads next.

## Strategic Implication

The homepage should not ask visitors to self-select too early.

Avoid a first screen that feels like:

- "For investors"
- "For recruiters"
- "For publishers"
- "For workshops"
- "For universities"

That would fragment Ax before the site has made the larger case.

Instead, the homepage should first establish the whole person:

1. identity
2. thesis
3. story pattern
4. proof
5. current work
6. doors for deeper intent

The persona-specific paths should appear after the main case has been made.

## Homepage Job

In the first 10 seconds, the visitor should understand:

- Ax is a Creative Scientist.
- He builds applied AI products and human-centered systems.
- His work spans research, product, design, technology, and company building.
- The breadth is coherent, not scattered.
- This is someone worth looking into.

In the first 60 seconds, the visitor should feel:

- seriousness

In the first 5 minutes, the visitor should be able to answer:

The site's central persuasion should be:

This is why investors, hiring leaders, publishers, universities, workshop buyers, teammates, and media people can all see different opportunities while still recognizing the same person.

## Risks

### Risk 1: Too Broad

If the site tries to serve everyone equally, it will feel unfocused.

Countermeasure:

Lead with the whole-person case, then route.

### Risk 2: Too Impressive, Not Intimate

If the site over-indexes on proof, it will feel like a resume.

Countermeasure:

Anchor proof in story, scenes, and worldview.

### Risk 3: Too Personal, Not Actionable

If the site over-indexes on biography, opportunity-makers may not know what to do.

Countermeasure:

Every story path needs a proof moment and action path.

### Risk 4: Too Polymath, Not Legible

If the site celebrates range without hierarchy, visitors will admire but not act.

Countermeasure:

Use the translation/lived-systems spine as the organizing logic.

### Risk 5: Too Branded, Not True

If "Creative Scientist" and "Everything is Designed" feel like slogans instead of lived philosophy, trust drops.

Countermeasure:

Define them through concrete stories and projects before asking visitors to adopt the language.
