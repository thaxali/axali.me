## Purpose

This document maps the jobs to be done, needs, abilities, values, and preferences of the sub-personas who collapse into the primary axali.me audience:

> The high-agency opportunity-maker.

These visitors have different titles, but they share one underlying behavior:

> They can use access, reputation, capital, institutional power, hiring authority, publishing channels, social trust, or cultural distribution to create meaningful opportunity for Ax.

The design task is not to make one separate site for each of them. The task is to understand their distinct jobs, then cross-cut those jobs into one coherent experience.

## Core Collapsed Persona

### The High-Agency Opportunity-Maker

This person can materially change Ax's trajectory. They may be:

- an investor
- a hiring leader
- a publisher, agent, or editor
- a workshop or course buyer
- a university host
- a future teammate
- a press, podcast, or conference producer
- a strategic partner
- a senior introducer or respected weak tie

They arrive with different surface questions, but the deeper question is:

> Is Ax worth spending my access, reputation, money, time, attention, or social capital on?

The site must help them answer yes.

## Universal Jobs To Be Done

These jobs appear across almost every sub-persona.

### Job 1: Orient Quickly

When I encounter Ax, I want to understand who he is without working too hard, so I can decide whether to keep paying attention.

Needs:

- fast identity clarity
- a simple role/positioning phrase
- a non-scattered explanation of range
- current work and relevance

Site implications:

- homepage must land in seconds
- "Creative Scientist" needs definition through proof, not just branding
- no early persona-picker that fragments the whole-person case

### Job 2: Test Coherence

When I see that Ax has many layers, I want to understand the pattern that connects them, so I can tell whether the breadth is power or noise.

Needs:

- narrative spine
- recurring motifs
- proof that the range compounds
- clear distinction between polymath depth and scattered generalism

Site implications:

- use the translation / lived-systems spine
- connect childhood stories to later work without forcing myth
- organize work by what it proves, not only chronology

### Job 3: Assess Credibility

When I consider creating an opportunity for Ax, I want enough evidence that he can deliver, so I do not risk my reputation on a weak signal.

Needs:

- credentials
- outcomes
- testimonials
- institutional proof
- artifacts
- numbers where verified
- specificity

Site implications:

- proof inventory must be modular
- project pages need outcomes and role clarity
- testimonials need source context
- research and product proof must be easy to verify

### Job 4: Assess Taste And Judgment

When I evaluate Ax, I want to feel his taste, restraint, and judgment, so I can trust him in ambiguous, high-stakes contexts.

Needs:

- strong curation
- mature story handling
- clear thinking
- visual/editorial taste
- no inflated claims
- no generic AI hype

Site implications:

- design quality is itself proof
- writing voice matters
- personal story must be precise and dignified
- "Everything is Designed" must show up in the site's own craft

### Job 5: Map Ax To My Opportunity

When I become interested, I want to see how Ax maps to my specific opportunity, so I can imagine the next step.

Needs:

- role-fit paths
- clear offers
- inquiry routing
- current availability / openness
- examples by context

Site implications:

- "Work With Me" router
- dedicated modules for invest, hire, publish, book, invite, join, partner
- AI agent prompts for persona-specific questions

### Job 6: Reduce Social Risk

When I might introduce, hire, invest in, publish, invite, or book Ax, I want to feel safe attaching my name to him, so I can act without regret.

Needs:

- trust
- specificity
- third-party validation
- professionalism
- clarity about what is public
- no overclaiming

Site implications:

- visible proof and references
- media kit / bio assets
- accurate claims
- polished contact pathways

### Job 7: Feel Future Potential

When I finish exploring, I want to believe Ax's best work may still be ahead, so I feel urgency to connect now.

Needs:

- current work
- future-facing thesis
- momentum
- open loops
- invitation to participate

Site implications:

- Seena/current work must show momentum
- writing/thinking should show an active intellectual agenda
- CTAs should invite serious next conversations

## Collapsed Persona Definition

### Name

The High-Agency Opportunity-Maker

### Description

A serious person with access, authority, or influence who is deciding whether Ax is worth betting on, backing, inviting, publishing, hiring, joining, introducing, or amplifying.

### Primary Situation

They have encountered Ax through a fragment: a meeting, referral, search, pitch, talk, essay, project, company, or social mention. They suspect there is something interesting here, but they need the site to make the full pattern legible.

### Core Job

When I encounter Ax, I want to understand the pattern, proof, and future potential behind his range, so I can decide whether to spend my access, reputation, money, time, attention, or social capital on him.

### Functional Needs

- understand who Ax is
- verify credibility
- map Ax to opportunity
- find proof
- route to next action
- share him accurately

### Emotional Needs

- feel curiosity
- feel trust
- feel that the breadth makes sense
- feel low risk acting on him
- feel a sense of discovery
- feel future potential

### Social Needs

- explain Ax to others
- look smart for discovering/supporting him
- avoid reputational risk
- make a high-quality introduction or decision
- affiliate with a person of taste and seriousness

### Decision Trigger

They act when they can say:

> I understand who Ax is, why the range matters, what he has done, what he can do next, and why introducing/backing/hiring/publishing/inviting him reflects well on me.

## Design Requirements For axali.me

### Requirement 1: The Site Needs A One-Sentence Carryable Explanation

Visitors need language they can repeat.

Working forms:

- Ax is a Creative Scientist who builds applied AI products and human-centered systems.
- Ax translates research, design, and AI into products and ideas people can use.
- Ax turns human complexity into systems, stories, products, and companies.

### Requirement 2: The Homepage Must Make The Whole-Person Case First

Before routing to investor / hiring / publishing / workshop paths, the homepage should establish:

- identity
- thesis
- story pattern
- proof
- current work
- next doors

### Requirement 3: Proof Must Be Organized By What It Proves

Not just:

- Apple
- Amazon
- UW
- Bilt
- Itau
- Seena

But:

- AI-native product judgment
- human-centered systems thinking
- accessibility and adaptive interfaces
- 0-to-1 product building
- research rigor
- public teaching / workshop ability
- story and worldview

### Requirement 4: The Site Needs A Router For Serious Intent

Potential router labels:

- Work With Me
- Start a Conversation
- Opportunities
- Collaborate

Routes:

- Invest / Partner
- Hire / Rare Roles
- Book a Workshop
- Invite to Speak
- Press / Media
- Publishing / Editorial
- Join / Build With Me
- Introduce Me

### Requirement 5: The Site Needs Shareable Assets

High-agency visitors often need to forward Ax to someone else.

Assets:

- short bio
- medium bio
- long bio
- one-paragraph intro
- headshots
- CV / resume
- selected project links
- speaker topics
- workshop menu
- publication list
- media kit

### Requirement 6: The AI Agent Should Handle Persona-Specific Depth

The static site should create the main conviction. The agent should support high-intent exploration.

Agent prompts:

- Explain Ax to an investor.
- Where would Ax fit at Anthropic or DeepMind?
- What book could Ax write?
- What workshops can Ax run?
- What lectures can Ax give?
- Why would someone join Ax's company?
- What is Ax's research background?
- What is the story behind "Everything is Designed"?
- What proof supports Ax's AI product work?
