## Working Frame

axali.me is not just a portfolio. It is a personal piece of the internet that makes Ax legible at full resolution.

The visitor problem:

> I can tell there is a lot here, but I need to understand the pattern, the proof, and whether I should bet on this person.

The site problem:

> Ax is encountered in fragments: founder, PhD, AI builder, designer, researcher, product leader, immigrant/refugee story, accessibility technologist, workshop leader, writer, and public thinker. The site needs those fragments to resolve into one coherent person.

The site should move visitors through:

1. Curiosity
2. Comprehension
3. Trust
4. Conviction
5. Action

Core site promise:

> Understand Ax: the story, the systems, the work, and the point of view.

Working narrative spine:

> Ax translates imagined or unresolved human needs into lived systems: spaces, interfaces, products, companies, workshops, research, and AI-native experiences.

## Audience Hierarchy

Not every visitor should get equal homepage weight. The primary audiences are the ones who can create major opportunity, reputation, distribution, or company momentum.

### Primary Audiences

1. Investors evaluating Ax and Seena
2. Hiring leaders for frontier AI, product, design, research, and technical leadership roles
3. Workshop, speaking, and course buyers
4. Publishers, agents, editors, and media gatekeepers
5. Future teammates and collaborators
6. University lecture, visiting scientist, and academic program hosts

### Secondary Audiences

7. Internet discoverers, weak ties, and old colleagues
8. HCI, AI, design, and research peers
9. Potential customers, design partners, and strategic partners
10. Students, mentees, and early-career builders
11. Personal community
12. AI agents, search systems, and automated summaries

## Persona 1: The Conviction Investor

### Who They Are

A VC, angel, scout, accelerator partner, or operator-investor evaluating Seena, Ax, or a future company.

### How They Arrive

- Warm intro from a founder, operator, or investor
- Seena pitch or deck
- LinkedIn / X / newsletter
- Search after meeting Ax
- A forwarded link from someone saying "you should know this person"

### Their Core Question

Is Ax the kind of founder who sees a non-obvious future, can build toward it, and can attract the people and market needed to make it real?

### What They Are Really Evaluating

- Founder-market fit
- Taste and judgment
- Depth of insight
- Ability to build product, team, and narrative
- Resilience without mythology
- Whether the range is an advantage or a distraction
- Whether Seena is backed by a founder with an unfair perspective

### Skepticism

- "Is this too broad?"
- "Is he a founder, consultant, researcher, or job seeker?"
- "Can he focus?"
- "Does he have distribution?"
- "Is this product insight or just strong storytelling?"

### Proof Needed

- Founder story that explains why Ax sees the problem differently
- Seena thesis and product truth
- Prior 0-to-1 product launches
- AI and HCI credibility
- Evidence of product taste
- Testimonials from serious operators
- Evidence he can recruit, teach, and inspire

### Desired Action

Request a meeting, make an intro, invest, or track Ax closely.

### Ideal Site Path

1. Homepage: immediately understands Ax as Creative Scientist and applied AI product builder.
2. Story spine: sees the pattern behind the range.
3. Work atlas: sees proof across research, big tech, startups, and AI.
4. Seena / current work: understands the company and founder-market fit.
5. Ask Ax agent: asks investor-specific questions.
6. Contact: warm CTA for investors and strategic partners.

### Experience Design Need

The investor path needs a "why this founder" page or module. It should not feel like a job candidate page. It should show inevitability: Ax's life and work explain why he would build this company.

## Cross-Persona Design Implications

### 1. One Story, Many Doors

The site should not become a separate microsite for each audience. It needs one coherent story with audience-specific doors.

Possible top-level doors:

- Story
- Work
- Thinking
- Speaking
- Ask Ax
- Work With Me

### 2. A "Work With Me" Router

The CTA problem is complex. Different visitors need different actions:

- invest
- hire
- book a workshop
- invite a lecture
- request press/media
- discuss publishing
- join Seena
- partner
- subscribe

A single "Work With Me" or "Start a Conversation" page can route these without crowding the homepage.

### 3. Press / Speaking / Workshops Should Be First-Class

This should not be a tiny footer. It should include:

- short bio
- medium bio
- long bio
- headshots
- speaking topics
- current talks
- past talks
- workshops
- sample formats
- past audiences / logos where allowed
- testimonials
- booking CTA

### 4. Publishing Needs Its Own Proof Layer

Publishing visitors need to see:

- narrative stakes
- point of view
- writing voice
- book concept lanes
- audience signals
- press/speaking credibility
- why Ax is not just an expert but a public thinker

This could live inside "Thinking" plus a "For Press and Publishing" subsection.

### 5. The AI Agent Is A Concierge For Depth

The static site should answer the first-order question: "Who is Ax?"

The agent should answer high-intent, persona-specific questions:

- Why should I invest in Ax?
- Where would Ax fit in a frontier AI lab?
- What workshops does Ax give?
- What lectures can Ax give at a university?
- What book could Ax write?
- What is Ax's research background?
- What is the story behind the blueprint / compass / interpreter app?
- What is his work on dyslexia / Parkinson's / AI?

### 6. Proof Needs To Be Modular

The same proof point should serve different personas:

- Dyslexia AI game: investor proof, research proof, university proof, human-centered AI proof, book proof.
- Apple mixed-reality work: frontier product proof, hiring proof, design proof.
- Adobe workshops: workshop proof, speaking proof, publishing/platform proof.
- Seena: founder proof, teammate proof, investor proof.

The site should tag proof by theme and audience rather than burying it chronologically.
